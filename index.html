Battle of Walaja
633 CE - undefined CE
Khalid ibn al-Walid used a double-envelopment (pincer) maneuver against Sasanian forces in Iraq — a tactically sophisticated victory that opened the road to the Euphrates valley.
Battle of Ajnadayn
634 CE - undefined CE
First major engagement between Muslim forces and the Byzantine army in Palestine. Abu Ubaydah ibn al-Jarrah and Khalid commanded. A decisive Muslim victory that opened Palestine to the Islamic conquests.
Battle of The Bridge (al-Jisr)
634 CE - undefined CE
A rare early Muslim defeat in Iraq. Muslim forces under Abu Ubayd al-Thaqafi were ambushed by Sasanian war elephants at a river crossing. Abu Ubayd was killed. The defeat temporarily halted the advance into Iraq.
Battle of Buwaib
635 CE - undefined CE
Major Muslim victory over Sasanian forces in Iraq under al-Muthanna ibn Haritha. Helped recover Muslim morale after the defeat at the Bridge and resumed the advance into the Sasanian heartland.
Battle of Yarmuk
636 CE - undefined CE
One of the most decisive battles in world history. Muslim forces under Khalid ibn al-Walid defeated the Byzantine army in modern Jordan/Syria. Opened Syria and Palestine permanently to Islamic rule. Byzantines never recovered these territories.
Siege and Surrender of Jerusalem
637 CE - undefined CE
Jerusalem surrendered peacefully to Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab. The Patriarch Sophronius insisted that only the Caliph himself receive the keys. Umar visited the Church of the Holy Sepulchre but refused to pray inside to protect it from being converted.
Battle of al-Jalula
637 CE - undefined CE
Muslim victory over Sasanian forces in Iraq, following al-Qadisiyyah. The Sasanian army was routed and retreated to their heartland in Persia. The road to Ctesiphon (al-Mada'in) was opened.
Battle of the Camel
656 CE - undefined CE
First civil war battle between Muslims. Caliph Ali ibn Abi Talib's forces defeated the coalition of Talha, Zubayr, and Aisha near Basra. Talha and Zubayr were killed. Aisha was escorted home by Ali. Named for Aisha's camel amid which fighting occurred.
Battle of Siffin
657 CE - undefined CE
Inconclusive battle between Caliph Ali's forces and Muawiyah's Syrian army. When Ali's forces were winning, Muawiyah's troops raised Qurans on spears calling for arbitration — a famous moment of political manipulation. Led to arbitration and eventually the Kharijite schism.
Battle of Nahrawan
658 CE - undefined CE
Ali ibn Abi Talib defeated the Kharijites — extremists who had seceded after accepting arbitration at Siffin. Though militarily victorious, the battle planted seeds of anti-Ali sentiment that led to his assassination.
Battle of Karbala
680 CE - undefined CE
The most pivotal and tragic event in Islamic history after the Prophet's death. Husayn ibn Ali, grandson of the Prophet, and 72 companions were killed by Yazid's Umayyad forces at Karbala, Iraq. Deeply split Islam into Sunni and Shia. Commemorated annually on Ashura.
Battle of al-Harrah
683 CE - undefined CE
Umayyad forces under Muslim ibn Uqbah attacked Medina after it rebelled against Yazid I. The city was sacked and the citizens subjected to three days of pillage. One of the most condemned events in early Islamic history.
Battle of Marj Rahit
684 CE - undefined CE
Marwan I defeated the Qaysi Arabs supporting Ibn al-Zubayr's rival caliphate. Established Marwanid Umayyad control over Syria. Deepened the tribal split between Qays (northern) and Yaman (southern) Arabs that would plague the Umayyads.
Battle of the Zab
750 CE - undefined CE
Abbasid forces under Abdullah ibn Ali defeated the last Umayyad Caliph Marwan II on the Greater Zab river in Iraq. Marwan II fled and was killed in Egypt. Ended the Umayyad Caliphate and established the Abbasid dynasty.
Battle of Debal — Conquest of Sindh
711 CE - undefined CE
Muhammad ibn Qasim's forces landed at Debal (near modern Karachi) and began the Muslim conquest of the Sindh region. Led to the first permanent Islamic state in the Indian subcontinent.
Battle of Guadalete — Conquest of Spain
711 CE - undefined CE
Tariq ibn Ziyad crossed from Morocco with 7,000 troops and defeated the Visigothic King Roderic at the Battle of Guadalete. Opened the Iberian Peninsula to the Muslim conquests. Within years, nearly all of Spain was under Muslim control.
Battle of Tours (Battle of Poitiers)
732 CE - undefined CE
Charles Martel's Frankish forces halted the northernmost Muslim advance into Europe near Poitiers, France. The Muslim commander Abd al-Rahman al-Ghafiqi was killed. Considered by European historians as a turning point that preserved Christian Europe.
Battle of Talas
751 CE - undefined CE
Arab Abbasid forces defeated the Tang Chinese army in Central Asia near the Talas River (modern Kyrgyzstan). Determined that Central Asia would remain Muslim, not Chinese. Chinese papermakers captured in the battle brought the technology of papermaking to the Islamic world.
Battle of Montgisard
1177 CE - undefined CE
Young Saladin's Egyptian army was surprised and routed by a much smaller Crusader force under King Baldwin IV (the Leper King) of Jerusalem. One of Saladin's rare defeats, demonstrating the Crusaders' fighting capability.
Battle of Hattin
1187 CE - undefined CE
Saladin's greatest victory. He destroyed the main Crusader army near the Sea of Galilee by denying them water, then attacked. Virtually the entire Crusader military force was annihilated. Jerusalem surrendered three months later. Triggered the Third Crusade.
Battle of Arsuf
1191 CE - undefined CE
Richard I of England defeated Saladin's forces near Arsuf on the Palestinian coast. Richard's disciplined army withstood a relentless assault before a devastating charge. Saladin's forces retreated with heavy losses.
Battle of Ain Jalut
1260 CE - undefined CE
Mamluk Sultan Qutuz and his general Baibars defeated the Mongol army under Kitbuqa in Palestine — the first major defeat of the Mongols in battle and one of the most significant in world history. Stopped Mongol expansion into North Africa and the rest of the Muslim world.
Siege of Acre — End of Crusades
1291 CE - undefined CE
Mamluk Sultan Khalil ibn Qalawun besieged and captured Acre, the last major Crusader stronghold in the Holy Land. The fall of Acre ended nearly 200 years of Crusader presence in the Levant.
Battle of Ankara
1402 CE - undefined CE
Timur (Tamerlane) defeated and captured the Ottoman Sultan Bayezid I near Ankara. Bayezid died in captivity. The Ottoman Empire nearly collapsed, entering the Interregnum (1402-1413). Timur's victory inadvertently prolonged the Byzantine Empire.
Battle of Varna

Ottoman Sultan Mehmed II (the Conqueror) captured Constantinople after a 53-day siege on May 29, 1453. The Byzantine Emperor Constantine XI died in the final battle. End of the Byzantine Empire (Eastern Roman Empire) after 1,100 years. Istanbul became the new Ottoman capital.

Ottoman Sultan Selim I defeated Safavid Shah Ismail I in northwestern Iran. The Ottomans' use of artillery and firearms proved decisive against the Safavid cavalry. Established the Ottoman-Safavid frontier roughly where it remains today.

Grand Vizier Kara Mustafa Pasha's massive Ottoman army besieged Vienna. A relief force under Polish King Jan III Sobieski and the Holy League broke the siege in the decisive Battle of Kahlenberg. Began the Ottoman retreat from Central Europe.

Prince Eugene of Savoy annihilated the Ottoman army while it was crossing the Tisa River, killing Grand Vizier Elmas Mehmed Pasha. The crushing defeat led to the Treaty of Karlowitz (1699) — the first major Ottoman territorial concessions.

Egypt and Syria launched a surprise attack on Israeli positions on Yom Kippur (October 1973). Initial Arab successes were reversed by Israeli counter-offensives. Led to the Camp David Accords (1978) and eventually Egypt-Israel peace (1979).

Battle of Guadalete
711 CE - 711 CE
The Battle of Guadalete took place in 711 CE in what is now southern Spain, near the Guadalete River in present-day Andalusia. This decisive battle marked the beginning of the Muslim conquest of the Iberian Peninsula. The Umayyad forces, led by Tariq ibn Ziyad with approximately 7,000 Berber and Arab troops, defeated the much larger Visigothic army of King Roderick (Rodrigo). The Visigothic kingdom, which had ruled much of Iberia for nearly three centuries, collapsed following this defeat and Roderick's death in battle. The victory opened the way for rapid Muslim expansion across the peninsula, leading to over seven centuries of Islamic rule in various parts of Spain. This battle fundamentally changed the religious, cultural, and political landscape of medieval Iberia.
